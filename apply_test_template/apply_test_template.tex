% ═══ Section: Welcome to Note-Taking! ═════════════════
\begin{sectionheader}
\emoji{🌟} \sectionfont{\textcolor{dark-text}{Welcome to Note-Taking!}}
\end{sectionheader}

\vspace{4pt}

Effective note-taking is crucial for understanding and remembering literature. Well-organized notes help you identify patterns, analyze characters, and understand themes more easily.

\vspace{4pt}

% Key Concepts Table
\noindent
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|>{\cellcolor{teal-light}\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{3.5cm}|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X|}
  \hline
  \textbf{\textcolor{dark-text}{What is Plot?}} &
  A \textbf{plot} is the sequence of events in a story. It typically includes an exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, and resolution. Understanding the plot helps in structuring notes chronologically. \\
  \hline
  \textbf{\textcolor{dark-text}{What is Character Analysis?}} &
  \textbf{Character analysis} involves examining a character's traits, motivations, and role in the story. Analyzing characters helps identify their impact on the plot and themes. \\
  \hline
  \textbf{\textcolor{dark-text}{What is Theme?}} &
  A \textbf{theme} is the central idea or message of a literary work. Themes are often universal truths about life, society, or human nature. \\
  \hline
  \textbf{\textcolor{dark-text}{What are Literary Devices?}} &
  \textbf{Literary devices} are techniques used by writers to create specific effects. Examples include metaphors, similes, and symbolism. \\
  \hline
\end{tabularx}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=2pt, topsep=4pt]
